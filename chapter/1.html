Chapter 1 - History

Cooper’s Ferry is an active prehistorical archaeological excavation site located along the Salmon River.The excavation’s primary focus is to find evidence of human occupation at this location. Originally opened in 1997 by Dr. Loren Davis, Cooper’s Ferry has been an ongoing seasonal excavation site since 2008. Every summer, between the month of June through August, Dr. Davis and his team of Oregon State University graduate and undergraduate students reopen the site and continue digging in hopes of finding evidence of the first humans to settle in North America.

Cooper’s Ferry is located in Northern Idaho, close to the Idaho-Oregon-Washington border. Specifically the Cooper’s Ferry excavation site is located alongside the Salmon River in the Lower Salmon River Canyon region. It can be found 15 miles west of Cottonwood, Idaho, directly at the intersection of Graves Creek and the Salmon River.

The Cooper’s Ferry excavation site and the Cooper’s Ferry Archaeological Field School are directly supported by two major entities: Oregon State University and the United Land Management. The site is also supported by several private supporters such as: the Keystone Archaeological Research Fund, the Bernice Peltier Huber Charitable Trust, and the National Geographic Society. Dr. Loren Davis, a professor of Anthropology at Oregon State University, is the lead archaeologist of the Cooper’s Ferry excavation site and the director and instructor of the field school. David Sisson is the head archaeologist for the Bureau of Land Management Coeur d’Alene district, and oversees the BLM interest of the Cooper’s Ferry site.
